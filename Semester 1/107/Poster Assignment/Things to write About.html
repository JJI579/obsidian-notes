Klarna Klarna is a Multi-choice payment plan creditor which provides interest free payment plans to contribute / benefit towards the customer’s payments without them having to pay the amount outright. Through this, Klarna act as a middle-man service for customers to pay back a temporary credit based on their purchases.

Properties: - Creditor - Provide loans out to provide a way for customers to receive items without paying in the full amount straight away - “BNPL” - A Buy Now Pay Later service provider, one amongst many other competitors.

Business Demands

Klarna meets the demands of their customer through providing an intuitive way to manage and maintain pricing for purchases made by customers, acting as a middleman between the majority of companies for payment plans.

Though their main selling point is their payment plans, the execution of the business to meet the demands of a variety of customer markets have made their business successful in many ways: 1. They have developed an intuitive app which is personalised with the customer’s details, preferences and data for customers to access and be kept up to date on their purchases 2. They have developed a streamline support centre to handle the majority of customer service chats without the requirement of a support agent, making a simple and intuitive system to users to handle disputes. They have understood their customer base by providing personalised recommendations based on prior searches, purchases and data provided via their third party vendors. Through this they have optimised the “feed” of content to keep customers enticed to their products. ## Ethical Considerations

Klarna as a business have to be cautious based on their market sector being based on temporary debt for their own customers, because of this there are many potential ethical considerations. Their business strategy is based on accumulating a debt for their customers until they receive payments due from the customers. Therefore the business can potentially downplay the idea of debt and proceed to inflict the chance of customers going into unmanageable debt The business will have to share details between third party vendors and customers, as well as handling and storing the data themselves. If not following the privacy policy designed by themselves and the third party vendor they could potentially breach privacy laws expected by the customers. Their advertisements towards the public have to be considerate of how lenient they can be on the idea of their business. As any sort of “downplay” towards their changed version of debt accumulation can be considered an unethical way to entice customers to use their service.

Paypal Credit is one amongst the other competitors Klarna have to compare against. Paypal Credit is also a BNPL-based provider and competes in the market for providing the accessible service that Klarna provides, however has benefits by different means. 1. They have longer payment plans including 4 months at 0% interest, and longer dependent on the purchase amount 2. Unlike Klarna, they cannot provide in-store purchases however are more devoted to the online-based service that they provide, such as e-commerce websites. 3. Fees on their services for businesses/corporate are lower to use at 1.2% which makes businesses more inclined to use them
