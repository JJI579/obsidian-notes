• How many times must a single die be rolled to guarantee that some number is obtained at least twice?
  • (b) How many times must two dice be rolled to guarantee that the same total score is obtained at least twice
  • How many times must two dice be rolled to guarantee that the same total score is obtained at least three times?

Use the pigeonhole principle to give solutions to the following problems:

How many times must a single die be rolled to guarantee that some number is obtained at least twice?

Considering there are 6 sides to the dice, and each of them are to be landed on once prior to receiving a new number, 6+1 would mean that it is guaranteed to receive a number of the same value is prior.

formula \(6*1+1\)

(b) How many times must two dice be rolled to guarantee that the same total score is obtained at least twice

Same consideration as prior, two 6-sided dice can be rolled

Considering each possibility of the die, there are 11 combinations as there is no possibility of receiving an answer of 1 due to there being to die with minimum values of 1 (1+1=2)

therefore to guarantee the same total score is obtained twice, it would be n+1 onto the existing 11 combinations resulting in 12.

How many times must two dice be rolled to guarantee that the same total score is obtained at least three times?

11 combinations for rolling the score twice with 2 die now wanting to obtain it 3 times \(11*2 = 22\) and therefore n+1 onto that is 23 and 23 possible combinations are required
