Use a naive Bayes’ classifier to predict whether your friend likes a Red Domestic SUV. Explain your computation in detail.

Yes = 5/10 No = 5/10

Red, Domestic, SUV | Yes

Red | Yes = \(\frac{3}{5}\) = 0.6 Domestic | Yes = \(\frac{2}{5}\) = 0.4 SUV | Yes = \(\frac{1}{5}\) = 0.2 Yes = \(\frac{1}{2}\) = 0.5 \(=0.024\)

Red | No = \(\frac{2}{5}\) = 0.4 Domestic | No = \(\frac{3}{5}\) = 0.6 SUV | No = \(\frac{3}{5}\) = 0.6 No = 0.5 = 0.072

therefore no

P(Likes = Yes) = 5/10 = 0.5 P(Likes = No) = 5/10 = 0.5 (or 1-P(Likes = Yes))

(Domestic, SUV, Red | Yes)

Assume Conditional Independence to total it

\(P(Domestic | Yes) * P(SUV | Yes) * P(Red | Yes) * P(Yes)\)

P(Domestic | Yes) = 2/5 P(SUV | Yes) = 1/5 P(Red | Yes) = 3/5 P(Yes) = 0.5

== 0.024

However we need No Also

\(P(Domestic | No) * P(SUV | No) * P(Red | No) * P(No)\)

P(Domestic | No) = 3/5 P(SUV | No) = 3/5 P(Red | No) = 2/5 P(No) = 0.5

== 0.072

Therefore

P(Yes | Domestic, SUV, Red) = 0.024 P(No | Domestic, SUV, Red) = 0.072

They are more likely to not like the red car
