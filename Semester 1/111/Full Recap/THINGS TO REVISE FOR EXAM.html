Agents - Topic

Narrow AI (Weak) - able to solve a small number of specific intellectual problems, no conciousness

AGI - Artificial General Intelligence, can accomplish any intellectual task that humans can perform

Most “AI” are technically Narrow AI

History in AI

Minsky and Edmonds 1951 - Built first neural network Turing 1950 - First chess playing programmes Dartmouth Conference 1956 - Coined the name, artificial Intelligence

Problems throughout the years (1970s) - Combinatorial Explosion - Lack of general technology to sustain storage / data levels required

Lighthill Report 1973 - Came to conclusion that combinatorial explosion cannot be addressed and the UK government ended support for AI research

IBM Deep Blue (chess engine) beat Garry kasparow 1997 - done because of technological advancements in storage etc

IBM Watson wins Jeopardy (2011) AlphaGo wins against 9-dan professional (2016) Libratus (CMU) wins in Poker (2017) AlphaFold: Predict 3D models of protein structures (2020)

Simple Reflex Agent

Actuator Sensor Environment

perceives something through sensor, detects what it is, compares in its system what it is, and returns its response through actuator

Condition-action rules No history

PEAS Descriptor

Performance Measure - Criteria to measure the success of a behaviour Environment Actuator Sensors

Environments

Fully observable vs partially observable - partial = more memory Deterministic vs stochastic = Deterministic = one step can be predicted what happens next Episodic vs sequential = Sequential = next step can affect all future decisions Static vs dynamic = Static = Unchanged while agent is deciding its response Discrete vs continuous = Discrete means it has a finite number of states

Model-based Agents

Has a history of one prior step Current perception + prior = current state

Goal-based Agents

Determines its factors of where it is trying to achieve more logic if the steps to achieve it is more long-winded Has history and operates similar to model-based agent

Utility-based Agents

Has a utility function to determine desirability from its next action tries to maximise its actions through the utility function

Doesn’t prioritise history - focuses on current and future states ## Learning Agents

Improves overtime through its past performances has history uses what its already learnt to perform an action evaluates outcomes and compares to desired performances
