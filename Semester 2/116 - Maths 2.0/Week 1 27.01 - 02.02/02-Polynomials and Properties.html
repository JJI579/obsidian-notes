In terms of polynomials

You cannot have anything < 0 or fractional as this impedes the idea of a polynomial

Why?? EG: \(x^{-2}\) = \(\dfrac{1}{x}\) and in accordance to polynomial rules, you cannot have a variable as the divisor. EG 2: \(x^{1/2} = \sqrt{ x }\) and this is not allowed as the exponent is fractional

What is not a polynomial?

\(\sqrt{ x }\) as the exponent (thing you are technically dividing by) is \(\dfrac{1}{2}\)

Polynomial Degree

the degree of a polynomial with only one variable is the largest exponent of that variable

\(4x^3 − x + 2\) - The degree of this polynomial is 3 as it is the largest exponent
